# Human Health Plan Foundation Officially Launches Global Health Data Sharing Platform

<b>Release Date</b>: 2025-12-18

## Core Content

The Human Health Plan Foundation today announced the official launch of the Global Health Data Sharing Platform, aiming to promote global health data sharing and cooperation, and accelerate the progress of medical research and disease treatment.

## Platform Vision

This platform will become an open sharing hub for global health data, connecting medical institutions, research institutions, and health organizations worldwide to achieve safe, standardized, and efficient sharing of health data.

## Technical Features

1. <b>Blockchain Technology</b>: Adopts blockchain technology to ensure data security and privacy protection
2. <b>AI-Driven Analysis</b>: Integrates artificial intelligence technology to provide intelligent data analysis and insights
3. <b>Standardized Interfaces</b>: Establishes unified data standards and interfaces to facilitate data exchange between different systems
4. <b>Multi-Level Permission Management</b>: Implements fine-grained permission control to ensure secure data access

## Cooperation Institutions

The first batch of institutions joining the platform includes:
- World Health Organization (WHO)
- National Institutes of Health (NIH)
- Chinese Academy of Sciences
- Medical Research Council (MRC)

## Future Plans

The platform plans to, within the next three years:
- Cover more than 50 countries and regions worldwide
- Include more than 10 million health data samples
- Support more than 100 key disease research projects
- Train more than 1,000 health data management and analysis talents

## Social Impact

The launch of the Global Health Data Sharing Platform will have a profound impact on global health initiatives:
- Accelerate medical research progress and shorten new drug development cycles
- Improve disease prediction and prevention capabilities
- Promote global health equity and narrow health gaps between different regions
- Drive innovation and development in the health industry
