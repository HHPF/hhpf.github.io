# Foundation Releases "2025 Global Health Report"

<b>Release Date</b>: 2025-12-10

## Report Overview

The Human Health Plan Foundation today released the "2025 Global Health Report", the Foundation's first global health status report. Based on health data from more than 100 countries and regions worldwide, the report analyzes the current global health situation, challenges, and proposes suggestions and directions for future health development.

## Key Findings

The report's key findings include:

1. <b>Overall improvement in global health</b>: Over the past decade, global life expectancy has increased from 71.4 to 73.8 years
2. <b>Prominent health inequality</b>: The gap in life expectancy between high-income and low-income countries remains 18 years
3. <b>Non-communicable diseases as the main threat</b>: 68% of global deaths are caused by non-communicable diseases
4. <b>Growing mental health issues</b>: Approximately 1 billion people worldwide are affected by mental health problems
5. <b>Health system resilience challenged</b>: The COVID-19 pandemic exposed the vulnerabilities of global health systems

## Policy Recommendations

The report proposes the following policy recommendations:

1. Strengthen global health governance and establish a more equitable health system
2. Increase investment in primary health care
3. Promote health technology innovation and digital transformation
4. Enhance health data sharing and cooperation
5. Promote health equity and narrow health gaps

## Social Impact

The release of the "2025 Global Health Report" will provide important references for governments, international organizations, and health institutions around the world, helping to promote the development of global health initiatives and achieve health-related goals in the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals.
